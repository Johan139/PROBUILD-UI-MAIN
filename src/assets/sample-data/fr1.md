### **High-End Project Context: Hernandez Residence**

A high-end build of the Hernandez Residence transforms the structure from a standard production home into a bespoke luxury property defined by superior materials, meticulous craftsmanship, and advanced systems. The client's expectations for this version are rooted in achieving a premium aesthetic, exceptional durability, and a high level of design sophistication. This involves sourcing unique or imported finishes, demanding precise execution from all trades, and ensuring the final product has a long, low-maintenance lifespan.

**Examples of Upgraded Finishes, Materials, and Methods:**

1. **Exterior Cladding:** Instead of standard builder-grade brick and stucco, the high-end version would feature hand-molded or reclaimed brick, natural quarried stone veneers (limestone or Lueders stone, common in high-end Texas homes), and a three-coat stucco system with integrated color for a durable, monolithic finish.
2. **Windows and Doors:** Upgrading from standard vinyl or aluminum-clad windows to high-performance, custom-fabricated steel or premium wood windows (e.g., Marvin Ultimate, Pella Architect Series) with advanced glazing for superior thermal and acoustic insulation. The front entry would be a custom-designed solid wood or iron door.
3. **Roofing System:** Moving beyond standard architectural shingles to a more permanent and visually striking solution, such as standing-seam metal roofing, natural slate tiles, or high-grade concrete tiles designed to mimic slate or clay.
4. **Interior Finishes:** Level 5 smooth drywall finish throughout, replacing the standard textured finishes. Flooring would be upgraded to wide-plank, site-finished hardwood, large-format porcelain tile, or natural stone instead of engineered wood or standard tile.
5. **Cabinetry and Millwork:** Custom-built, site-fitted cabinetry in the kitchen and bathrooms featuring premium woods, inset doors, and high-end hardware, replacing semi-custom or stock options. Baseboards, casings, and crown moldings would be upgraded to solid wood with more intricate profiles.
6. **Countertops:** Use of full-slab natural stone like quartzite or marble for kitchen islands and master bathroom vanities, including mitered waterfall edges, instead of standard granite or quartz with simple edge profiles.
7. **MEP Systems:** Installation of a high-efficiency, multi-zone HVAC system with smart thermostats and humidity control. The electrical system would include a comprehensive home automation system (e.g., Control4, Savant) for lighting, security, and AV control, along with designer-grade fixtures and outlets.
8. **Insulation:** Upgrading from standard batt insulation to a full encapsulation with open-cell or closed-cell spray foam insulation, creating a superior thermal envelope for energy efficiency and sound deadening.

This premium approach significantly impacts the project's key metrics. The **budget** will increase substantially due to higher material costs, the need for specialized labor, and longer lead times for custom or imported items. The **schedule** will be extended to accommodate the fabrication of custom components and the more meticulous installation processes required for high-end finishes. Finally, **contractor selection** becomes critical; a general contractor with a proven portfolio of luxury custom homes and established relationships with high-end trade partners is essential to meet the elevated expectations for quality and execution.
